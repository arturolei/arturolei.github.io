ABOUT

I am an aspiring web developer, Python programmer, trivia aficionado and avid consumer of pulp fiction and Sherlock Holmes pastiches. I am currently based in that marvelous city of ideas, innovation, and insights: Berkeley, CA.

I defected from the Humanities (Italian Studies) to the Sciences and then to the private sector. I had been doing research on 19th century Italian imaginings of the future. I then joined a lab at UC Berkeley exploring the future of medicine. Now I work in a tech-lite, academia-adjacent position for a firm where I help shape how knowledge and cutting-edge research are shared around the world. (By the way, we're hiring!)

I have been an active member and contributor of the Italian Society at Berkeley, a student group at UC Berkeley dedicated to sharing Italian culture in the Bay Area and strengthening connections between the Bay Area and Italy.

I am a not-too-infrequent collaborator on/contributor to Codebuddies.org, an open-source community that brings beginner programmers and developers together.

I now and then co-host trivia nights at a local pizzeria, Bobby G's Pizzeria, in Berkeley, CA.

PROJECTS:

Personal Book Catalog

I am working on creating a web-based cataloging system of all my books (current estimate: 1000+ and growing!);the current rough build can be found on Github and is based mostly the tutorial project for the MDN Django tutorial. I opted to use Django to gain experience in how to use a "battieres-included" development framework. This project will also prove an excellent opportunity to work with a "real" database.

Planned Features:

  • Mobile-ready input forms for easy on-the-go cataloging
  • Searchable Catalog (so I don't end up with yet another copy of Gone Girl or Watchmen)
  • Basic metadata about the book in question (How and when was an item acquired? Borrowed or not? ISBN?)
